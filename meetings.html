Meeting Overview

Date: November 4, 2025

Governing body: City Council

Meeting type: Regular Session

Record basis: This recap is based on the approved City Council meeting minutes.

Items Approved

  • Approval of the consent agenda, including:
    • October 21, 2025 Open Session Council Minutes
    • October 23, 2025 Special Session Council Minutes
    • Bill No. 2025-77 (Relocation of Liberty Utility power poles at the Recycle Center – easement)
    • Bill No. 2025-80 (Amending City Code related to franchise tax/gross receipts)
    • Bill No. 2025-83 (Agreement for the Big Spring Park Master Plan)
  • Amendment to contract with Liberty Utilities
  • Sale of surplus Fire Department ATVs to Hale Fire Protection District for $7,500
  • Acceptance of the bid from Lost Creek Construction, LLC for new pavilion/restroom at Scenic Park ($73,375)
  • Approval of Bill No. 2025-84: Agreement with Allgeier, Martin and Associates, Inc. for Hwy 59 improvements (up to $88,000)
  • Approval of Bill No. 2025-85: Amendment to MoDOT NPE grant for Neosho Airport T-Hangar (additional grant funds and match)
  • Approval of Bill No. 2025-86: Acceptance of MoDOT IIJA grant for Neosho Airport T-Hangar and local match
  • Approval of Bill No. 2025-87: Ratifying Resolution No. 2025-41 on surplus government property
  • Approval of Resolution Bill No. 2025-38: MOU with Area Agency on Aging for Senior Center support
  • Approval of Resolution Bill No. 2025-39: MOU with Neosho Fire Department and Camp Crowder
  • Approval of Resolution Bill No. 2025-41: Declaring certain Police Department vehicles as surplus
  • Approval of request for water connection for property at 1353 Neosho Drive

Items Rejected

None.

Items Amended or Changed

The meeting agenda was amended to remove one item under New Business prior to approval.

Items Still in Progress

Chapter 100 bond request for a business facility expansion, which staff reported is under development and will be brought back to Council for future consideration.

What to Watch Going Forward

Future Council consideration of a Chapter 100 bond agreement once staff work with legal counsel is complete.

Source Note

This summary is based on the meeting agenda, agenda packet, and approved City Council meeting minutes. The minutes are treated as the final authority for all actions and outcomes.
