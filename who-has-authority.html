Who Has Authority to Do What

Understanding the separation of roles in Neosho city government.

One of the most common sources of frustration in local government is confusion about who actually has the authority to make decisions.

Key principle: Authority in Neosho is intentionally divided. No single person or body controls everything — and that’s by design.

City Council (Legislative Authority)

City Council is the legislative body of the City. Council acts collectively in public meetings and exercises authority only through votes.

  • Adopts ordinances and resolutions
  • Sets policy and long-term direction
  • Approves the City budget
  • Appoints the City Manager and boards/commissions
What City Council does not do
  • Run daily operations
  • Direct individual staff members
  • Decide enforcement outcomes
  • Prejudge appeals, hearings, or variances

City Manager (Administrative Authority)

The City Manager is responsible for administering the City’s operations and carrying out the policies adopted by City Council.

  • Supervises City staff and departments
  • Implements Council-adopted policy
  • Oversees enforcement of ordinances
  • Manages contracts, services, and daily operations

Boards & Commissions (Limited / Quasi-Judicial Authority)

Certain boards and commissions are established by ordinance or the Charter to handle specific matters, often involving hearings or appeals.

  • Act within narrowly defined authority
  • Must follow due-process requirements
  • Do not take direction from individual Council members

City Staff (Operational Authority)

City staff carry out the day-to-day work of the City under the direction of the City Manager.

  • Apply and enforce adopted ordinances
  • Follow established procedures
  • Provide professional recommendations
Why this separation matters: It protects fairness, prevents favoritism, ensures due process, and shields the City from legal and financial risk.

A helpful rule of thumb

If a question is about policy, it usually belongs to City Council. If it’s about execution or enforcement, it usually belongs to the City Manager and staff.
